Overview

Korean popular music (K-pop) has become incredibly popular not only in Korea and the rest of Asia but also all around the world. Let's explore how it became this way!

Timeline of Significant Events

First Generation K-pop

The 1990s was the genesis of modern K-pop. K-pop artists incorporated American pop-style elements into their music and wanted to cater to the teenage audience. This led to the emergence of idol boy and girl bands, which laid the foundation of the popularity of idol bands today. H.O.T. became one of the first boy bands, and they were wildly popular. Their cheerful music and lyrics resonated with the teenage audience. Fans would even also attempt to replicate the members' clothing and hairstyles! The chart below shows H.O.T.'s album sales.

The 90s also introduced three large entertainment companies that would later dominate the Korean entertainment industry: SM Entertainment, YG Entertainment, and JYP Entertainment.

Although K-pop looked like it was beginning to take off, the market would slump in the early 2000s, and many of the popular groups from the 90s went on the decline or even disbanded.

Second Generation K-pop

By the mid-late 2000s, K-pop began to make a re-emergence. In 2006, the boy band Big Bang debuted under YG Entertainment. Although their debut did not start them off with a bang, a string of hit singles would propel them to become one of the most popular bands in K-pop history. They were the first big K-pop hip hop group, and really started the spread of K-pop and Korean culture around the world. Because of Big Bang's success, YouTube launched its own K-pop channel to recognize K-pop's emerging popularity world-wide.

During this time, another soon-to-be titan would rise up as well. Female singer and actress IU debuted in 2008. IU was and still is an anomaly - in an age of idol bands, she dominates the K-pop industry as a solo-act. Her soprano vocal range is iconic, and her music generally differs from mainstream K-pop styles. Many solo artists in the K-pop industry do not last that long, but IU has stood strong in the test of time. Although she may not have as much international recognition as some other K-pop artists, domestically, she is one of, if not the most, popular idol in Korea.

K-pop Today

Today, the most well-known K-pop group is BTS. BTS debuted in 2013, but has taken the world by storm in recent years. BTS made its way into mainstream American music in 2017, when it became the first Korean group ever to win a Billboard Music Award as the Top Social Artist. BTS is the best selling artist in Korean history, and continues to be one of the most popular groups in the world. Their music often touches on many personal and social themes such as mental health, loss, and individualism. BTS's fanclub, ARMY, is also known to be very outspoken on social media.

Another incredibly popular K-pop group today is BLACKPINK. They first debuted in 2016. BLACKPINK has colloborated with some big names in the West such as Dua Lipa and Selena Gomez. They have an immense online presence, as they became the first group ever to have more than 1 billion views on 4 separate music videos on YouTube. Each of the members also have a very large Instagram following as well, as they are the most followed individuals from Korea.
